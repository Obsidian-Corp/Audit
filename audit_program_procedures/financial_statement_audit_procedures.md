# Financial Statement Audit Procedures

## Program Overview

**Program Type:** Financial Statement Audit
**Industries:** Healthcare, Financial Services, Technology, Manufacturing, General
**Objective:** To obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error, and to issue an auditor's report that includes the auditor's opinion.

**Standards:** Conducted in accordance with Generally Accepted Auditing Standards (GAAS), including the Statements on Auditing Standards (SAS) issued by the AICPA.

## Key Audit Areas

### 1. Cash & Cash Equivalents

#### 1.1 Bank Reconciliations Review (FSA-100)
**Objective:** Verify accuracy and completeness of bank reconciliations

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain all material bank account reconciliations at year-end
2. Review reconciliations for mathematical accuracy
3. Investigate outstanding items over 90 days old
4. Test that reconciling items clear in subsequent period (review bank statements for 30 days post year-end)
5. Verify proper approval and review sign-offs on reconciliations
6. Reperform reconciliation for largest bank account
7. Document findings and any unexplained variances

**Sample Size:** All material accounts (>5% of total cash)
**Evidence Required:** Bank reconciliations, bank statements, outstanding check lists, cleared check images
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Stale outstanding checks (over 6 months)
- Unexplained reconciling items
- Lack of proper approval/review
- Math errors in reconciliation
- Deposits in transit not clearing within 3-5 business days

#### 1.2 Bank Confirmations (FSA-101)
**Objective:** Confirm cash balances directly with financial institutions

**Procedures:**
1. Prepare standard bank confirmation requests for all accounts
2. Obtain client authorization signatures
3. Mail confirmations directly to financial institutions with return envelope to auditor
4. Follow up on non-responses with second and third requests
5. Reconcile confirmed amounts to general ledger balances
6. For non-responses, perform alternative procedures:
   - Review bank statements
   - Review subsequent cash receipts and disbursements
   - Verify mathematical accuracy of reconciliations

**Sample Size:** All bank accounts (100% coverage)
**Evidence Required:** Completed bank confirmations, bank statements if alternative procedures needed
**Estimated Hours:** 3 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Non-responses requiring alternative procedures
- Differences between confirmed amounts and GL
- Accounts not disclosed to auditors
- Restrictions on accounts not properly disclosed

#### 1.3 Cash Cutoff Testing (FSA-102)
**Objective:** Ensure cash transactions are recorded in the proper accounting period

**Procedures:**
1. Review bank statements for last 5 business days before year-end
2. Review bank statements for first 5 business days after year-end
3. Select 10-15 deposits around cutoff and trace to cash receipts journal
4. Select 10-15 disbursements around cutoff and trace to cash disbursements journal
5. Verify transactions are recorded in the proper period
6. Review for any unusual year-end transactions
7. Test that receipts deposited before year-end are recorded as cash, not receivables

**Sample Size:** 10-15 transactions per direction (deposits and disbursements)
**Evidence Required:** Bank statements, cash receipts journal, cash disbursements journal, deposit slips
**Estimated Hours:** 2 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Deposits made before year-end but not recorded until next period
- Checks written before year-end but not recorded
- Window dressing - artificial manipulation of cash balances

### 2. Accounts Receivable

#### 2.1 AR Aging Analysis (FSA-200)
**Objective:** Analyze accounts receivable aging and assess collectibility

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain detailed AR aging report at year-end
2. Foot and cross-foot aging to verify mathematical accuracy
3. Reconcile aging total to general ledger AR balance
4. Analyze aging trends by comparing to prior periods
5. Calculate days sales outstanding (DSO) and compare to industry benchmarks
6. Identify concentration of receivables from single customers (>10% of total)
7. Review credit terms compliance and identify past due amounts
8. Interview management about collection efforts for aged balances
9. Assess overall collectibility and adequacy of allowance

**Sample Size:** Full aging analysis
**Evidence Required:** AR aging report, general ledger detail, prior period comparisons, collection correspondence
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Receivables concentrated with few customers
- Large balances in >90 day aging buckets
- DSO trending upward
- Credit terms not aligned with actual payment patterns
- Lack of collection efforts on aged balances

#### 2.2 AR Confirmations (FSA-201)
**Objective:** Confirm existence and accuracy of receivable balances with customers

**Procedures:**
1. Select sample of customer accounts for confirmation (positive confirmations)
2. Sample should include:
   - All balances >materiality threshold
   - Random selection to cover 60-70% of total AR
   - All related party balances
   - Unusual balances (credit balances, very old balances)
3. Prepare confirmation requests on client letterhead
4. Mail confirmations directly to customers with return envelope to auditor
5. Follow up on non-responses with second requests at 2 weeks
6. Investigate and resolve exceptions/differences
7. For non-responses, perform alternative procedures:
   - Review subsequent cash receipts
   - Examine supporting invoices and shipping documentation
   - Review customer orders and contracts

**Sample Size:** Material balances covering 60-70% of total AR
**Evidence Required:** Confirmation responses, invoices, shipping documents, cash receipts, sales orders
**Estimated Hours:** 6 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- High non-response rates (>30%)
- Exceptions indicating timing differences or disputes
- Systematic differences suggesting cut-off issues
- Unauthorized credits or adjustments

#### 2.3 Allowance for Doubtful Accounts (FSA-202)
**Objective:** Assess adequacy of bad debt allowance

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain management's calculation of allowance for doubtful accounts
2. Understand methodology (percentage of sales, aging percentages, specific identification)
3. Test mathematical accuracy of calculation
4. For percentage method:
   - Review historical collection rates by aging category
   - Assess whether historical rates are still appropriate
   - Compare to industry benchmarks
5. For specific identification:
   - Review customer-specific collection issues
   - Assess reasonableness of estimated losses
   - Review correspondence with customers
6. Analyze write-off history for past 2-3 years
7. Review subsequent collections (30-60 days after year-end)
8. Assess current economic conditions and industry trends
9. Compare allowance as % of AR to prior periods and industry

**Sample Size:** Full reserve calculation review
**Evidence Required:** Allowance calculation, write-off history, collection data, aging reports, subsequent cash receipts, customer correspondence
**Estimated Hours:** 5 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Allowance methodology not supported by historical data
- Failure to adjust for current economic conditions
- Specific reserves not supported by evidence
- Write-offs not timely processed
- Allowance too low relative to aging and collection history

#### 2.4 Revenue Recognition Testing (FSA-203)
**Objective:** Test compliance with revenue recognition standards (ASC 606)

**Procedures:**
1. Review revenue recognition policies for compliance with ASC 606
2. Identify distinct performance obligations in contracts
3. Test sample of revenue transactions (30-40 transactions across revenue streams):
   - Review contract or purchase order
   - Verify performance obligations are identified
   - Confirm performance obligations are satisfied before revenue recognition
   - Verify revenue amount is properly calculated
   - Review shipping/delivery documentation
   - For services, review evidence of service delivery
   - For multi-element arrangements, test allocation to performance obligations
4. Test that revenue recognition timing is appropriate
5. Review significant contracts for unusual terms
6. Test that contract assets and liabilities are properly recorded
7. Verify proper disclosure of revenue recognition policies

**Sample Size:** 30-40 transactions across revenue streams
**Evidence Required:** Sales contracts, invoices, shipping records, proof of delivery, revenue recognition memos, ASC 606 analyses
**Estimated Hours:** 6 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Revenue recognized before performance obligations satisfied
- Improper allocation in multi-element arrangements
- Bill-and-hold arrangements not meeting criteria
- Side agreements modifying terms
- Variable consideration not properly estimated

#### 2.5 AR Cutoff Testing (FSA-204)
**Objective:** Test proper period cutoff for revenue and receivables

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain sales journal for last 10 days before year-end
2. Obtain sales journal for first 5 days after year-end
3. Select 15-20 transactions before year-end:
   - Review invoice, shipping document, and delivery confirmation
   - Verify goods shipped and invoiced before year-end
   - Confirm recorded in proper period
4. Select 15-20 transactions after year-end:
   - Review invoice, shipping document, and delivery confirmation
   - Verify goods not shipped until after year-end
   - Confirm not recorded as revenue in prior period
5. Review for any unusual year-end transactions or patterns
6. Test that year-end accrued revenue is supported by delivery before year-end

**Sample Size:** 15-20 transactions per direction
**Evidence Required:** Invoices, shipping logs, bills of lading, proof of delivery, sales orders
**Estimated Hours:** 3 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Premature revenue recognition (goods not shipped)
- Shipping terms not properly understood (FOB shipping point vs. destination)
- Bill-and-hold transactions
- Year-end sales spikes or unusual patterns
- Channel stuffing

### 3. Inventory

#### 3.1 Physical Inventory Observation (FSA-300)
**Objective:** Observe physical inventory count procedures and verify existence

**Procedures:**
1. Review client's physical inventory count instructions and procedures
2. Assess adequacy of count procedures and controls
3. Attend physical inventory counts at material locations
4. Observe count procedures:
   - Pre-count preparation and organization
   - Count teams and segregation of duties
   - Two-count procedures and reconciliation
   - Identification of damaged/obsolete items
   - Control over count tags/sheets
   - Handling of inventory movements during count
5. Perform independent test counts of 30-50 items
6. Record test count information for subsequent trace testing
7. Observe cutoff procedures (last receipts/shipments)
8. Review count discrepancies and how they are resolved
9. After counts, trace test counts to final inventory listing (both directions)

**Sample Size:** 30-50 items for test counts
**Evidence Required:** Count sheets, test count documentation, final inventory listing, count instructions
**Estimated Hours:** 8 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Inadequate count procedures or lack of two-count system
- Inventory not properly organized
- Count teams not properly trained
- Obsolete or damaged items not identified
- Poor cutoff procedures
- Significant count adjustments

#### 3.2 Inventory Valuation Testing (FSA-301)
**Objective:** Test inventory valuation at lower of cost or net realizable value

**Procedures:**
1. Select sample of 25-30 significant inventory items
2. For each item, test cost:
   - Trace to purchase invoices for purchased goods
   - Review cost accumulation for manufactured goods
   - Verify proper cost flow method (FIFO, average cost, etc.)
   - Test overhead allocation for manufactured inventory
3. Test net realizable value (NRV):
   - Review recent sales prices
   - Review current price lists
   - Research market prices for raw materials
   - Assess selling costs
4. Compare cost to NRV and verify lower amount is used
5. Identify slow-moving items (no movement in >6 months)
6. Review obsolescence indicators:
   - Technology changes
   - New models/versions
   - Excess inventory levels
   - Damaged goods

**Sample Size:** 25-30 significant items
**Evidence Required:** Cost records, purchase invoices, recent sales invoices, price lists, market data, movement reports
**Estimated Hours:** 5 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Cost exceeds NRV without write-down
- Overhead allocation rates not supported
- Slow-moving items not identified
- Obsolete inventory not reserved
- LIFO layers from many years ago

#### 3.3 Inventory Obsolescence Reserve (FSA-302)
**Objective:** Assess adequacy of obsolescence reserve

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain management's obsolescence reserve calculation
2. Review methodology for determining obsolescence
3. Analyze slow-moving inventory reports:
   - Items with no sales in 6, 12, 24 months
   - Items with excess quantities vs. projected usage
4. Review subsequent sales of year-end inventory:
   - Items sold at discounts
   - Items with no subsequent movement
5. Interview management about:
   - Product life cycles
   - Technology changes
   - New models superseding old inventory
6. Test reserves for specifically identified obsolete items
7. Assess overall reasonableness of reserve as % of inventory
8. Compare to prior periods and industry benchmarks

**Sample Size:** All reserved items and slow-moving analysis
**Evidence Required:** Obsolescence calculation, movement reports, sales data, product life cycle analysis, subsequent sales
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Obsolete items not identified or reserved
- Reserves not based on evidence
- Slow-moving inventory not addressed
- Methodology changed without justification
- Inventory in excess of projected needs

#### 3.4 Inventory Cutoff Testing (FSA-303)
**Objective:** Test proper cutoff for inventory purchases and sales

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain last receiving report number before year-end
2. Obtain last shipping document number before year-end
3. Select 10-15 receiving reports before cutoff:
   - Verify goods received before year-end are in inventory
   - Verify corresponding payable is recorded
   - Review receiving report, purchase order, and invoice
4. Select 10-15 receiving reports after cutoff:
   - Verify goods received after year-end are excluded from inventory
   - Verify payable not recorded until next period
5. Select 10-15 shipments before cutoff:
   - Verify goods shipped are excluded from inventory
   - Verify revenue and receivable are recorded
6. Select 10-15 shipments after cutoff:
   - Verify goods not yet shipped are included in inventory
   - Verify revenue not recognized

**Sample Size:** 10-15 transactions per direction
**Evidence Required:** Receiving reports, shipping documents, purchase orders, sales invoices
**Estimated Hours:** 3 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Goods in transit not properly accounted for
- Shipping terms misunderstood (FOB point)
- Inventory counted but not received (or received but not counted)
- Shipments made but not removed from inventory

### 4. Fixed Assets

#### 4.1 Fixed Asset Additions (FSA-400)
**Objective:** Test additions to fixed assets for existence, valuation, and proper classification

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain schedule of fixed asset additions for the year
2. Foot schedule and agree to general ledger
3. Select sample of 15-20 significant additions
4. For each addition:
   - Review invoice and verify amount capitalized
   - Assess capitalization vs. expense per company policy
   - Verify asset exists through physical inspection or other evidence
   - Review asset classification (building, equipment, vehicles, etc.)
   - Verify placed-in-service date is accurate
   - Verify depreciation began in proper period
5. Review capital budget and investigate major variances
6. Assess whether repairs/maintenance expense contains capitalizable items

**Sample Size:** 15-20 significant additions
**Evidence Required:** Fixed asset register, invoices, capital budgets, photos/inspection notes
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Repairs expensed when should be capitalized
- Assets capitalized when should be expensed
- Assets not yet placed in service but being depreciated
- Improper classification affecting depreciation
- Missing supporting documentation

#### 4.2 Fixed Asset Disposals (FSA-401)
**Objective:** Test disposals and retirements of fixed assets

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain schedule of asset disposals/retirements for the year
2. Select all material disposals for testing
3. For each disposal:
   - Verify asset was removed from asset register
   - Review disposal documentation (sale agreement, scrap receipt, etc.)
   - Verify asset no longer owned or in use
   - Recalculate gain/loss on disposal
   - Verify proper recording in financial statements
4. Review for assets that should be retired but remain on books
5. Perform walkthrough of facilities to identify retired assets still on register

**Sample Size:** All material disposals
**Evidence Required:** Disposal documentation, sales agreements, asset register, gain/loss calculations
**Estimated Hours:** 3 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Fully depreciated assets still in use not retired
- Disposed assets not removed from register
- Gain/loss calculation errors
- Assets sold but not properly documented

#### 4.3 Depreciation Testing (FSA-402)
**Objective:** Test depreciation calculations and useful life estimates

**Procedures:**
1. Review company's depreciation policies and useful lives
2. Compare useful lives to IRS guidelines and industry norms
3. Select sample of 20-25 assets across different classes
4. For each asset:
   - Recalculate annual depreciation expense
   - Verify proper depreciation method (straight-line, declining balance, etc.)
   - Verify placed-in-service date
   - Assess reasonableness of useful life
5. Test that fully depreciated assets are not depreciated further
6. Test depreciation for assets placed in service mid-year (half-year convention)
7. Review for indicators of impairment

**Sample Size:** 20-25 assets across classes
**Evidence Required:** Depreciation schedules, asset policies, calculations, useful life support
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Incorrect useful lives
- Depreciation method not per policy
- Calculation errors
- Assets depreciated before placed in service
- Salvage value not properly considered

#### 4.4 Leased Assets - ASC 842 (FSA-404)
**Objective:** Test lease accounting compliance with ASC 842

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain complete population of leases
2. Assess completeness by reviewing:
   - Rent expense accounts
   - Contracts and agreements
   - Management representation
3. For each material lease:
   - Review lease agreement
   - Verify lease classification (operating vs. finance)
   - Test right-of-use asset calculation
   - Test lease liability calculation
   - Verify discount rate is appropriate (incremental borrowing rate)
   - Test lease term determination (including renewal options)
   - Verify initial direct costs included in ROU asset
4. Test current vs. long-term classification of lease liability
5. Verify proper financial statement presentation and disclosure

**Sample Size:** All material leases
**Evidence Required:** Lease agreements, lease calculations, discount rate support, lease classification analysis
**Estimated Hours:** 5 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Embedded leases not identified
- Improper lease classification
- Discount rate not supportable
- Renewal options not properly evaluated
- Variable lease payments not properly treated

### 5. Accounts Payable & Liabilities

#### 5.1 AP Cutoff Testing (FSA-501)
**Objective:** Test proper period cutoff for purchases and payables

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain receiving log for last 10 days before year-end
2. Obtain receiving log for first 5 days after year-end
3. Review vendor statements received post year-end
4. Select 15-20 receiving reports before year-end:
   - Verify goods received before year-end
   - Verify corresponding liability is recorded
   - Match to purchase order and invoice
5. Select 15-20 receiving reports after year-end:
   - Verify goods received after year-end
   - Verify liability not recorded in prior year
6. Review open purchase orders at year-end for unrecorded liabilities

**Sample Size:** 15-20 transactions per direction
**Evidence Required:** Receiving reports, invoices, purchase orders, vendor statements
**Estimated Hours:** 3 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Goods received but not recorded as payables
- Payables recorded for goods not yet received
- Timing differences on vendor statements
- In-transit inventory

#### 5.2 Search for Unrecorded Liabilities (FSA-502)
**Objective:** Identify unrecorded liabilities at year-end

**Procedures:**
1. Review cash disbursements for 30-60 days after year-end
2. For significant disbursements, determine if related to pre year-end obligation:
   - Review invoice date
   - Review service/delivery period
   - Assess if should have been accrued
3. Review open purchase orders at year-end
4. Review receiving reports for period after year-end
5. Review large or unusual post year-end invoices
6. Search for unrecorded professional fees (legal, audit, consulting)
7. Review board minutes for commitment to expenditures
8. Inquire of management about known obligations

**Sample Size:** 30-60 days post year-end disbursements
**Evidence Required:** Subsequent disbursements, open purchase orders, receiving reports, invoices, board minutes
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Professional fees not accrued
- Utilities and services not accrued
- Year-end bonuses not accrued
- Warranty obligations not recorded
- Legal settlements not recorded

#### 5.3 Accrued Liabilities Testing (FSA-503)
**Objective:** Test accrued liability balances for completeness and accuracy

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain schedule of accrued liabilities at year-end
2. Common accruals to test:
   - Accrued payroll and benefits
   - Accrued vacation
   - Accrued professional fees
   - Accrued utilities
   - Accrued interest
   - Accrued taxes
3. For each material accrual:
   - Review calculation methodology
   - Test mathematical accuracy
   - Verify assumptions are reasonable
   - Review subsequent payment to verify accuracy
4. Compare accruals to prior year and investigate variances
5. Assess completeness of accruals

**Sample Size:** All material accruals
**Evidence Required:** Accrual schedules, calculations, invoices, contracts, subsequent payments
**Estimated Hours:** 3 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Accruals not properly calculated
- Missing accruals for known expenses
- Prior year accruals not properly reversed
- Vacation accrual not based on current rates

#### 5.4 Debt Obligations Testing (FSA-505)
**Objective:** Test debt balances, terms, and covenant compliance

**Procedures:**
1. Obtain schedule of all debt obligations
2. Send confirmation requests to all lenders
3. For each debt obligation:
   - Confirm balance with lender
   - Review debt agreement for terms and covenants
   - Test interest rate and payment terms
   - Verify current vs. long-term classification
   - Calculate debt covenant compliance
   - Review compliance certificates if required
4. Test interest expense calculation
5. Test debt issuance costs and amortization
6. Verify proper disclosure of debt terms, rates, maturities, and covenants

**Sample Size:** All debt obligations (100% coverage)
**Evidence Required:** Loan agreements, debt confirmations, compliance calculations, amortization schedules
**Estimated Hours:** 4 hours

**Common Issues to Watch For:**
- Debt covenant violations not disclosed
- Current portion not properly classified
- Interest expense not properly accrued
- Debt issuance costs not properly amortized
- Related party debt not disclosed

## Documentation Requirements

### Required Work Papers
1. Planning documentation:
   - Risk assessment
   - Materiality calculations
   - Audit strategy
2. Internal control documentation and testing results
3. Substantive testing work papers for each financial statement area
4. Analytical review procedures
5. Management representations letter
6. Subsequent events review
7. Going concern assessment
8. Summary of uncorrected misstatements
9. Financial statement review checklist
10. Engagement quality review documentation

### Evidence Standards
- All work papers must be signed and dated by preparer
- All work papers must be reviewed and signed by reviewer
- Evidence must be sufficient and appropriate
- Electronic evidence should be retained in audit file
- Cross-references between work papers required
- Conclusions must be clearly documented

## Deliverables

### Primary Deliverables
1. **Auditor's Report** - Opinion on financial statements
2. **Audited Financial Statements** - With auditor's report
3. **Management Letter** - Internal control observations and recommendations
4. **Required Communications Letter** - SAS 114/115 communications to those charged with governance

### Timeline Considerations
- Planning phase: 2-3 weeks
- Interim procedures: 3-4 weeks (if applicable)
- Year-end fieldwork: 4-6 weeks
- Review and finalization: 2-3 weeks
- Total engagement: 10-15 weeks typical

## Common Findings and Red Flags

### High Risk Areas
1. Revenue recognition - timing and completeness issues
2. Inventory valuation - obsolescence not identified
3. Allowance for doubtful accounts - inadequate reserves
4. Cut-off issues - transactions recorded in wrong period
5. Related party transactions - not identified or disclosed
6. Going concern issues - not properly assessed
7. Fraud indicators - unusual transactions or patterns
8. Internal control deficiencies - material weaknesses

### Fraud Indicators to Watch
- Unusual journal entries, especially at period end
- Significant related party transactions
- Revenue recognized without proper support
- Management override of controls
- Pressure to meet financial targets
- Unusual or complex transactions near period end
- Lack of segregation of duties
- Missing or altered documentation

## Sample Size Guidance

### Statistical Sampling Approach
- Confidence level: 95% typical
- Tolerable error: Performance materiality
- Expected error: Based on prior experience

### Non-Statistical Sampling
- All material items (>planning materiality)
- Sufficient to cover 60-80% of account balance
- Representation across different types/categories
- High-risk items
- Unusual items

## Risk Assessment Factors

### Inherent Risk Factors
- Industry conditions
- Complexity of transactions
- Management integrity
- Prior audit findings
- Economic conditions
- Related party relationships
- Geographic dispersion

### Control Risk Factors
- Quality of internal controls
- Segregation of duties
- Management oversight
- IT general controls
- Prior control deficiencies
- Control environment assessment
